1. THE ORIGINAL OF GOVERNMENT.

    _ CHAP. I. Who was the first Founder, or the first that instituted the Office of KINGS.

    _ CHAP. II. Whether the People can make a King or not.

    _ CHAP. III. What is meant by Anoynting of KINGS.

    _ CHAP. IV. Why they are called the Lords Anointed.

    _ CHAP. V. Whether bad Kings be the Lords Anointed, or not.

    _ CHAP. VI. Whether upon any pretences whatsoever it be lawful to depose, murder, or so much as to touch the Lords Anointed.

    _ CHAP. VII. What is meant by touching the Lords Annointed, or stretching forth the hand against the Lords Anointed.

    _ CHAP. VIII. Whether Kings now adays are to be had in the same veneration and esteem, as Kings were under the Law, by reason of our Christian liberty.

    _ CHAP. IX. Whether a King failing in his duty and not performing those things, which he hath sworn unto at his Coronation (so solemnly) the Peo•le are not disobliged in their obedience unto him, and may, thereupon, depose or put him to death.
